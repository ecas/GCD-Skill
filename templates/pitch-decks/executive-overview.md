# Pitch Deck Template: Executive Overview
# Audience: CxO (CEO, COO, CDO, CISO, CIO)
# Length: 10–12 slides | Target duration: 30–45 minutes
# Use when: First executive alignment meeting, EBC session, strategic review

---

## Slide 1 — Title

**Headline:** Partnering with {Company} to Lead in the Cloud Era

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Subtitle | A Strategic Conversation — {Month} {Year} |
| Your name | {AE Name}, {Title}, Google Cloud |
| Customer logo | [Insert {Company} logo] |
| Google logo | [Standard Google Cloud lockup] |

**Speaker notes:**
> Thank them for their time. State your agenda in one sentence: "Today I want to understand where {Company} is headed and share how Google is helping companies like yours get there faster." Do not start selling. Start listening.

**Timing:** 1 minute

---

## Slide 2 — Agenda

**Headline:** Thirty Minutes Well Spent

| # | Topic | Time |
|---|-------|------|
| 1 | Your challenges — what we've heard | 5 min |
| 2 | Where the industry is heading | 5 min |
| 3 | How Google approaches this differently | 8 min |
| 4 | What peers are seeing (proof) | 5 min |
| 5 | A potential path forward | 5 min |
| 6 | Open questions & next steps | 5 min |

**Speaker notes:**
> Set the agenda and confirm it. Ask: "Is there anything you'd like to add or re-order?" This gives executives ownership of the conversation. If they change the agenda, that tells you their priorities.

**Timing:** 1 minute

## Slide 3 — Your Challenges (Pain Point 1)

**Headline:** {Pain Point 1 — e.g., "Infrastructure Costs Are Growing Faster Than the Business"}

**Content structure:**
- **What we're hearing:** Short verbatim-style quote or paraphrase from discovery call
- **Why it matters now:** Business consequence (time, cost, risk, competitive)
- **Root cause:** Technology or process gap driving the pain

**Visual:** Single powerful stat or quote block (sourced from discovery or industry data)

**Speaker notes:**
> This slide should feel like you've done your homework. Reference something specific from your research or prior conversations. If you don't have specifics, use the industry baseline and ask: "Does this resonate with what you're seeing?" Silence here is information.

**Timing:** Part of combined pain-points discussion (slides 3–5): 5 minutes total

## Slide 4 — Your Challenges (Pain Point 2)

**Headline:** {Pain Point 2 — e.g., "Talent Is Hard to Hire and Even Harder to Retain"}

**Content structure:**
- **What we're hearing:** [Discovery insight or industry stat]
- **Why it matters now:** [Business impact]
- **Root cause:** [Underlying driver]

**Visual:** Icon + brief supporting data point

**Speaker notes:**

## Slide 5 — Your Challenges (Pain Point 3)

**Headline:** {Pain Point 3 — e.g., "Security and Compliance Requirements Are Multiplying"}

**Content structure:**
- **What we're hearing:** [Discovery insight or regulatory driver]
- **Why it matters now:** [Board-level / regulatory pressure]
- **Root cause:** [Fragmented tooling, legacy architecture, skills gap]

**Visual:** Regulatory timeline or risk matrix (optional)

**Speaker notes:**
> End the pain section with a summary question: "Of these three, which one is keeping you up at night?" Their answer shapes the rest of the conversation. Mark their answer mentally — return to it in the solution section.

**Timing:** See slide 3 combined timing

## Slide 6 — Industry Trends

**Headline:** The Companies Winning in {Industry} Are Making Three Bets

**Content structure:**
- **Trend 1:** [e.g., "AI-native operations over AI-assisted workflows"]
  - Implication for {Company}: [One sentence]
- **Trend 2:** [e.g., "Platform consolidation over best-of-breed sprawl"]
  - Implication for {Company}: [One sentence]
- **Trend 3:** [e.g., "Data as a competitive asset, not a cost center"]
  - Implication for {Company}: [One sentence]

**Source references:** [Gartner / IDC / Google-commissioned research — cite year]

**Speaker notes:**
> Frame these trends as observable facts, not Google opinions. Use third-party citations. The goal is for the executive to agree with the trends before you connect them to your solution. "Would you agree these trends are shaping your industry?" is the check question.

**Timing:** 5 minutes

## Slide 7 — Vision (Future State)

**Headline:** What {Company} Looks Like in Three Years — If You Move Now

**Content structure:**

| Today | In 3 Years |
|-------|-----------|
| {Current state description 1} | {Future state outcome 1} |
| {Current state description 2} | {Future state outcome 2} |
| {Current state description 3} | {Future state outcome 3} |

**Anchor statement:** "{Company} has the assets, the talent, and the market position. The question is the platform."

**Speaker notes:**
> Paint a picture. Use language specific to their industry and strategic priorities. Don't mention Google yet. Let them react to the vision. If they push back, calibrate. If they lean in, you have alignment. Move to the solution only after they acknowledge the vision.

**Timing:** Part of solution overview section (slides 7–8): 8 minutes total

## Slide 8 — Solution Overview

**Headline:** How Google Connects the Dots — From Pain to Outcome

**Content structure:**

| Your Pain | Google Capability | Your Outcome |
|-----------|------------------|--------------|
| {Pain Point 1} | {GCP/Workspace product or capability} | {Measurable outcome} |
| {Pain Point 2} | {GCP/Workspace product or capability} | {Measurable outcome} |
| {Pain Point 3} | {GCP/Workspace product or capability} | {Measurable outcome} |

**Visual:** Simple three-column table or flow diagram

**Speaker notes:**
> This is the pivot point from problem to solution. Map directly to the pains identified in slides 3–5. Do not introduce products that don't connect to confirmed pains. Less is more at CxO level — three clean connections beat ten scattered ones.

## Slide 10 — ROI Snapshot

**Headline:** The Financial Case, in Plain Language

**Content structure:**
- **Typical investment range:** [{Tier} engagement — details in detailed business case]
- **Expected return areas:**
  - Infrastructure savings: [e.g., "20–40% typical for comparable migrations"]
  - Productivity gains: [e.g., "10–15% from Workspace consolidation per IDC study"]
  - Risk reduction: [e.g., "Avoided cost of breach / compliance fine at {regulatory framework}"]
- **Payback period:** [Typical range for this profile — do not fabricate]
- **Full business case:** Available as a follow-on deliverable

**Speaker notes:**
> Do not go deep on numbers at this stage — you don't have enough data yet. The goal is to establish that there is a financial case and that you can quantify it. Offer the business case as a next step. If they push for numbers now, give ranges and caveat them clearly: "These are industry benchmarks — we'll refine with your actual data."

**Timing:** Part of next steps section (slides 10–12): 5 minutes total

## Slide 11 — Implementation Path

**Headline:** A Proven Path — Three Phases, Managed Risk

**Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–{n}):**
- Key activities: [e.g., Assessment, security baseline, first workloads]
- Milestone: [e.g., "First 20% of workloads migrated, security posture established"]
- Investment: [Low / contained]

**Phase 2 — Scale (Months {n}–{n}):**
- Key activities: [e.g., Core workload migration, data platform, Workspace rollout]
- Milestone: [e.g., "Majority of production workloads running on GCP"]
- Investment: [Primary spend phase]

**Phase 3 — Innovate (Months {n}+):**
- Key activities: [e.g., AI/ML use cases, analytics, competitive differentiation]
- Milestone: [e.g., "First AI-powered product feature in production"]
- Investment: [Ongoing, variable by initiative]

**Speaker notes:**
> Phasing addresses the executive's fear of big-bang risk. Emphasize that each phase delivers standalone value — they don't have to commit to all three to start. The phased approach also gives procurement a smaller initial PO to approve.

**Timing:** See slide 10 combined timing

## Usage Notes

- Replace all `{placeholders}` before presenting. Never present with visible placeholders.
- Slide count can be reduced to 10 by merging slides 3–5 into a single three-column pain overview.
- For Polish-language delivery: translate all narrative content; keep product names in English.
- Speaker note timing targets assume a 45-minute meeting. For a 30-minute meeting, cut slides 6 and 10.
